ICML 2024 Workshop on Accessible and Efficient Foundation Models for Biological Discovery 

This workshop seeks to bring machine learning (ML) and biology experts together to identify interdisciplinary approaches to evolve foundation ML models that could be used under constrained computational resources. While most cutting-edge ML architectures, such as the state-of-the-art protein language models, require large compute clusters and hundreds of GPU hours to train, most biological labs have access to much more modest compute resources, hence limiting the widespread adoption of such models. The workshop will provide a unique opportunity to expose the ICML community to the challenges and opportunities of integrating novel methods for training foundation and frontier artificial intelligence (AI) models into resource-constrained biological research. It will also highlight emerging trends in learning-based foundation models with limited resources and their implications for computational advancements in biological research. We anticipate that our workshop will highlight the pressing need for accessible and efficient foundation models in biology.

AccML.bio will be an in-person workshop at ICML 2024 in Vienna, Austria on July 27, 2024.

Call for Papers

We are seeking original submissions in topics including, but not limited to:

  • Parameter-, memory-, and compute-efficient foundation models for biological data
  • Algorithms for training efficient generative models in biology
  • Efficient fine-tuning and adaptation of biological foundation models
  • Accessible cloud/web-based methods for foundational biological discovery
  • Foundation model knowledge distillation and transfer learning across biological contexts
  • Model compression and quantization techniques for biological foundation models
  • Uncertainty modeling and hypothesis-driven machine learning in biology

Submissions

Submissions should be made through OpenReview.
